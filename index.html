Biography

I am a geoarchaeologist and anthropologist who uses methods and tools from the geosciences to study past and present human societies. I specialize in building models of coupled natural-human systems, focusing on the dynamic feedbacks between population growth, food production, and environmental change over the past 10,000 years. I am currently a postdoc in the Past Landscapes Lab at the University of Arizona, working to reconstruct hydroclimate variability in the western United States over the past millennium.
