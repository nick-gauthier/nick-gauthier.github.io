Biography

I am an anthropologist, geographer, and Earth system scientist who uses methods and tools from the geosciences to study past and present human societies. I specialize in building models of coupled natural-human systems, focusing on the feedbacks between population growth, food production, and climate change over the past 10,000 years.

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Scripps Institution of Oceanography at the University of California San Diego, working with Jade d’Alpoim Guedes to reconstruct climate during the domestication and dispersal of rice in east and southeast Asia, as well as an affiliate researcher at the University of Arizona School of Geography, Development and Environment. I was previously a postdoc at UA SGDE and the Laboratory of Tree Ring Research, working with Kevin Anchukaitis to reconstruct hydroclimate variability in the western United States over the past millennium. I received my PhD with Michael Barton from the School of Human Evolution and Social Change at Arizona State University.

Starting January 2022, I will be the Assistant Curator of Artificial Intelligence for Biological and Cultural Diversity at the Florida Museum of Natural History at the University of Florida. I am seeking creative graduate students and postdocs in geography or anthropology who want to use machine learning and agent-based modeling to answer questions at the intersection of the social and natural sciences.
